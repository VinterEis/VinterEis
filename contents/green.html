15 Ways to Reduce Global Warming and be More Green

Plant More Trees and Support Reforestation Efforts

You can plant more plants or trees in your home or land and help communities out there to plant more trees. The more trees are planted, the more CO2 emissions reduced and also the better air quality will be.

Use More Renewable Energy Sources

Renewable energy like wind, solar, and hydro produces little to no greenhouse gas emission compared to fossil fuels, also it's lower on energy cost. It's important for the whole world to make a fast transition to renewable energy. Installing solar panels or wind turbines in your home is a good effort to reduce or get rid of greenhouse emission.

Save Energy and Improve Its Efficiency

Turn off your electronics while not being used, use appliance and lightning with high energy efficiency, Keep heating and cooling systems well-maintained for optimal performance, etc. This small approach is absolutely really important to avoid climate change.

Don't Waste Your Food

When food is goes to landfill, it produces greenhouse gases such as methane that is more potential than CO2. That's why you need to abstain from wasting your food.

Adopt Green Building Practises

Adopting green building practices involves integrating strategies that minimize environmental impact and enhance sustainability throughout a building's lifecycle. This includes using energy-efficient systems, sustainable materials, and water-conserving fixtures, as well as incorporating renewable energy sources and green roofs. Emphasizing indoor air quality, efficient insulation, and sustainable site development further supports eco-friendly goals. Achieving certifications like LEED or BREEAM can validate these efforts, while ongoing education ensures the adoption of the latest sustainable technologies and practices.

Support Sustainable Businesses

With supporting Sustainable Bussinesses like investing in climate action and choose sustainable products or services, help them to do more actions to save the Earth.

Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

Reduce is to minimize the amount of waste generated and the consumption of resources. Reuse is to extend the life of products and materials to reduce the need for new items. Meanwhile Recycle is to process used materials into new products to reduce waste and conserve resources.

Waste-to-Energy

Convert organic waste into energy through processes like anaerobic digestion or waste incineration.

Don't make a smoke or burn things

This is necessary to avoid CO2 and other gas emission to produced.

Use Eco-Friendly Transportation

Drive less, switch to hybrid or electric vehicles, use public transportation, or even biking and walking are the best option for travelling short distance. With these you can reduce or stop amounts of CO2 produced.

Eat More Veggies

The more you eat veggies, the more plants will be planted, and if you eat less meat, the less CO2 will be produced.

Support Eco-Friendly Products

Buy Sustainable Products: Choose products made from recycled or sustainable materials. Avoid Single-Use Plastics: Option for reusable bags, bottles, and containers.

Offset Emissions

Invest in carbon offset programs to counterbalance your personal emissions. or you can use browser like Ecosia to help them plants more trees.

Track Your Carbon Footprint

Use online tools like UN Carbon Footprint Calculator to measure and understand your carbon footprint.

Educate and Influence Others

Educate and influence others is to make everyone understand to take actions to save our planet.
