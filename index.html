Facts About Antarctica

The southernmost continent, Antarctica, is a realm of extremes: vast ice sheets, frigid temperatures, and rich biodiversity. It's a vital hub for global scientific research on climate change and biodiversity, protected by the Antarctic Treaty System since 1959. Despite its harsh environment, Antarctica faces increasing challenges from climate change and human impact, highlighting the urgent need for conservation efforts to safeguard its unique ecosystems and global environmental stability.
